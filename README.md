Strategic Overview:

As organizations transition from domestic mid-market operations to complex global footprints, they frequently encounter severe FX exposure shock that threatens operating margins. 

This repository serves as the quantitative risk engine and architectural playbook for a hypothetical $250,000,000 Premium E-Mobility manufacturer aggressively scaling to a $500M global footprint. 

Instead of treating liquidity management and currency hedging as separate silos, this Python architecture proves how modern transaction banking mechanisms and ISO 20022 APIs can naturally reduce external derivative trading volumes and optimize global working capital.

Quantitative Python Architecture:

To demonstrate agile execution without reliance on legacy enterprise software or vulnerable manual spreadsheets, this risk analytics engine is built entirely in Python utilizing the `pandas` and `numpy` data libraries. 

The models included in this repository actively calculate:

* Parametric Portfolio Value at Risk (VaR): Mathematically proving a $352,533.13 reduction in maximum potential downside loss via layered forward hedging.
* Working Capital Optimization: Modeling a Supply Chain Finance (SCF) framework to generate millions in unlocked free cash flow.
* In-House Bank (IHB) & VAM Architecture: Mathematically proving a $282,000 reduction in redundant annual bank fees and unlocking $2,000,000 in trapped cash by transitioning to a centralized Virtual Account Management (VAM) framework for POBO/COBO efficiency.
* Macroeconomic Cost of Carry: Utilizing the Interest Rate Parity formula to evaluate margin erosion driven by divergent central bank policies across high-yield emerging markets.
* Asymmetric FX Options: Simulating derivative payoffs to protect uncommitted European sales pipelines.
